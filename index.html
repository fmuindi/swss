WHC Kids Harvest Sidewalk Sunday School

Bringing Church
to the Community

Sidewalk Sunday School is a community outreach ministry dedicated to bringing hope, joy, and the love of Jesus directly into neighborhoods across the Columbus area — through free family events filled with worship, games, food, giveaways, and meaningful moments of connection.

What Is SWSS?

More Than an Event.
It's Ministry in Motion.

Sidewalk Sunday School exists to reach children and families outside the four walls of the church. Through community events filled with fun, worship, encouragement, and practical support, we create opportunities for families to experience God's love in a real and personal way.

"Every event is designed to be welcoming, uplifting, and completely free for the community."

We believe the church belongs in every neighborhood — and Sidewalk Sunday School is how we live that out. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a regular face in the crowd, you are seen, loved, and welcome here.

What to Expect

Every Sidewalk Sunday School event is packed with activities designed to make families feel welcomed, fed, and filled with joy.

Food & Snacks

Fresh meals and snacks provided free for every family and child who attends.

Games & Activities

Inflatables, interactive games, fun competitions, and hands-on activities for all ages.

Face Painting & Giveaways

Exciting experiences and surprise giveaways that kids look forward to at every single event.

Worship & Bible Moments

Faith-filled moments of worship and storytelling designed for children and families.

Community Connection

A place where families feel truly seen, loved, encouraged, and connected to something greater.

Community Impact

Reaching Families One
Neighborhood at a Time

Sidewalk Sunday School is about more than hosting events. It's about building relationships, serving communities, and creating moments where children and families encounter hope. We go where people are — into streets, schools, and neighborhoods — to bring the love of God to every family we meet.

"Every smile, every conversation, every meal, and every prayer matters."
